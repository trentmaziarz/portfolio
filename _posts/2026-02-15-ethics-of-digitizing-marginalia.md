---
title: "On the Ethics of Digitizing Marginalia"
date: 2026-02-15
---

When we transcribe handwritten notes in the margins of old books, whose voice are we amplifying and whose are we erasing?

This is a question I've been sitting with as I work through a collection of annotated early modern texts. The marginalia are fascinating, evidence of real readers engaging with ideas in real time. But the act of digitizing them raises questions about selection, interpretation, and the power dynamics of who gets to be remembered.
